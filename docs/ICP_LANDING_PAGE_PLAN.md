# Zolara ICP Landing Page Plan

**Purpose:** Identify the 5 highest-signal ICP personas for the current Zolara direction and translate them into pain-relief positioning for the landing page.

**Current product direction:** Telegram-native AI consensus engine for groups that are too large, distributed, or socially uneven for meetings, polls, and group chats to produce real alignment. The strongest near-term beachhead is the 200-person creative/community campus pattern, with expansion paths into event organizing, member governance, onboarding, expert contribution, and emergency/volunteer coordination.

---

## Landing Page Positioning Spine

### Primary promise
**Stop running your community on whoever showed up. Zolara privately asks every member, synthesizes what they actually think, and shows you the decisions, tensions, and blind spots that need action.**

### Core proof points to show repeatedly
- **Private 1v1 Telegram conversations** remove public-pressure dynamics and do not require a new app.
- **Synthesis reports** turn raw replies into common ground, tensions, blind spots, and next steps.
- **Iterative rounds** deepen unclear topics instead of pretending one poll equals consensus.
- **Onboarding/Fresh Eyes capture** collects role, skills, interests, communication style, and newcomer observations before they disappear.
- **Admin status surfaces** (`/my_status`, planned `/dashboard`) clarify what is blocked: onboarding, validation, responses, or synthesis.
- **Managed project bots** make each community/project feel dedicated, branded, and context-aware.

## ICP 1 — Marta: Community Manager / Campus Operator

**Who she is:** Runs a 100-300 person creative campus, residency, co-living community, maker space, or member-based cultural hub.

**Concrete pain**
- Meetings cannot represent the community: 30-40 people attend, 150+ remain invisible.
- Decisions are shaped by hallway conversations, loud voices, and whoever complained last.
- She spends too much time chasing input, reminders, notes, recaps, and action items.
- When people disagree later, the legitimacy attack is predictable: “I was never consulted.”

**Pain-relief positioning**
- “Know what the whole community thinks before you make the call.”
- “Replace gut-feeling governance with a private async listening round.”
- “Turn 200 scattered opinions into one clear map: agreement, tensions, blind spots, next steps.”

**Outcome to promise**
- Higher participation than town halls or Telegram polls.
- More legitimate decisions because quiet members had a low-friction channel.
- Fewer surprise conflicts because tensions surface before they become public blowups.

**Product proof points**
- Alignment Round: bot DMs members privately in Telegram.
- Synthesis report: common ground + creative tensions + blind spots + proposed next steps.
- Reaction/validation loop: group can mark aligned, discuss, or disagree.
- Dashboard direction: admin can see onboarded vs pending, responses received vs missing, and next recommended action.

**Landing page angle**
- Hero/use-case section: “You can’t put 200 people in a room. You can still hear all of them.”
- Demo: studio allocation round with invisible accessibility blind spot surfaced.

## ICP 2 — Jānis: Event / Festival / Program Organizer

**Who he is:** Coordinates recurring events, festivals, volunteer teams, workshops, or community programs inside a larger member base.

**Concrete pain**
- Volunteer recruitment happens through ignored group chats and one-by-one DMs.
- Skills are hidden: he only knows what the usual 20 people can do.
- Creative input is bottlenecked by the people already close to the organizer.
- Post-event learning disappears because everyone is exhausted and no debrief happens.

**Pain-relief positioning**
- “Find the people, ideas, and risks hiding in your community before the event breaks.”
- “Collect creative input and volunteer capacity without another spreadsheet.”
- “Run the debrief while the memory is fresh — privately, async, and structured.”

**Outcome to promise**
- Better volunteer matching and fewer last-minute staffing gaps.
- More representative event concepts because ideas come from the whole community.
- Repeat mistakes decrease because post-event wisdom becomes a synthesis, not a forgotten chat thread.

**Product proof points**
- Brainstorm/Co-creation round: collects and clusters ideas.
- Onboarding profile: role, interests, availability, communication style, skills direction.
- Question personalization: asks different members based on profile/context.
- Synthesis: ranked themes, repeated blockers, suggested next actions.

**Landing page angle**
- Section: “From group-chat chaos to an idea map and volunteer signal.”
- Demo: festival planning round clustering 73 ideas into program, food, kids, sustainability, and logistics themes.

## ICP 3 — Leo: Quiet / Underrepresented Member

**Who he is:** Deeply affected by decisions but unlikely to speak in town halls, public Telegram threads, or direct confrontation with leadership.

**Concrete pain**
- Public settings reward confident speakers, not necessarily the best information.
- He has context on real problems but no safe, low-drama channel to share it.
- Existing feedback tools feel either too public, too shallow, or like complaining.
- He rarely sees evidence that his input changed anything.

**Pain-relief positioning**
- “Participate without performing.”
- “Say what you actually think privately, on your own time.”
- “See your perspective reflected in the group synthesis without being put on stage.”

**Outcome to promise**
- More authentic input from members who would never speak publicly.
- Less domination by social cliques or meeting regulars.
- Higher trust because members can see their concerns represented in reports.

**Product proof points**
- Private 1v1 Telegram questions.
- Optional anonymity/attribution modes.
- Validation prompts that check understanding before synthesis.
- Personal status surface (`/my_status`) showing contribution and current next step.

**Landing page angle**
- Member-facing strip: “No meeting. No public argument. Two thoughtful questions in Telegram.”
- Demo: member answers at 11pm; later sees the theme reflected anonymously in the report.

## ICP 4 — Ava: Veteran Contributor / Informal Steward

**Who she is:** Long-time member who carries invisible labor: events, conflict smoothing, onboarding, cleanup, crisis response, institutional memory.

**Concrete pain**
- The same reliable people absorb every operational gap.
- “Flat” governance gives her responsibility without authority or recognition.
- Her contribution is real but not portable: no proof for grants, jobs, residencies, or leadership roles.
- She wants to step back without letting the community collapse.

**Pain-relief positioning**
- “Make invisible contribution visible — without turning seniority into hierarchy.”
- “Distribute responsibility beyond the same five people.”
- “Give trusted members process privileges based on sustained contribution, not loudness.”

**Outcome to promise**
- Burnout pressure decreases as more members are pulled into structured participation.
- Veterans gain recognition and clear stewardship pathways.
- Communities preserve institutional memory instead of storing it in exhausted people’s heads.

**Product proof points**
- Member progression concept: contribution points, Trusted Voice, Domain Steward, Governance Circle.
- Credible Contribution Experience direction: exportable proof of sustained engagement.
- Project memory direction: past rounds/reports become searchable context.
- Steward privileges direction: trigger pulse checks or suggest rounds in a domain.

**Landing page angle**
- Section: “Recognize the people already holding the place together.”
- Proof module: contribution timeline → level → process unlocks → certificate/export.

## ICP 5 — Emergency / Volunteer Coordinator

**Who they are:** NGO, mutual aid, crisis response, or civic organizer coordinating 50-500 volunteers under time pressure.

**Concrete pain**
- Too many people arrive with unknown skills, availability, locations, and safety constraints.
- Critical updates get buried in noisy chats.
- Coordinators cannot manually check wellbeing, fatigue, incidents, and handovers at scale.
- Fast decisions need structured signal, not deep consensus.

**Pain-relief positioning**
- “Turn spontaneous volunteers into a structured response network.”
- “Know who can help, when, where, and with what — without interviewing everyone manually.”
- “Capture shift handovers and wellbeing signals before they become safety failures.”

**Outcome to promise**
- Faster volunteer intake and assignment.
- Fewer safety gaps from missing briefings or fatigue.
- Better continuity across shifts through synthesized handovers.

**Product proof points**
- Rapid onboarding/intake via Telegram 1v1.
- Per-shift cycles and low-depth operational questions.
- Safety and burnout monitoring from the use-case configuration model.
- Report/synthesis engine adapted to operational handover instead of governance consensus.

**Landing page angle**
- Secondary vertical, not the primary homepage beachhead yet.
- Use as “Zolara also works when the group is temporary, high-stakes, and fast-moving.”

## Recommended Landing Page Structure

1. **Hero — Marta-first**
   - Headline: “What if every decision reflected the people who didn’t show up?”
   - Subtitle: “Zolara privately interviews your community in Telegram, then synthesizes what they agree on, where they clash, and what nobody noticed.”
   - CTA: “Run an alignment round” / “See the Telegram demo”

2. **Problem — concrete scale pain**
   - “Town hall: 35/200.”
   - “Telegram poll: 23 votes.”
   - “Kitchen conversations: loudest voices win.”
   - “Decision backlash: ‘I wasn’t consulted.’”

3. **How it works — user experience, not architecture**
   - Admin chooses a topic.
   - Members answer privately in Telegram.
   - Zolara validates and synthesizes.
   - Group sees agreement, tensions, blind spots, next steps.

4. **Use-case modules**
   - Community governance: studio allocation/noise policy.
   - Event planning: festival brainstorm + volunteer signal.
   - Onboarding/Fresh Eyes: capture newcomer observations.
   - Pulse Check: detect satisfaction drops early.

5. **Member trust section**
   - Private input.
   - Anonymity options.
   - No meeting obligation.
   - Your contribution shows up in the synthesis.

6. **Proof/product section**
   - Managed project bot.
   - 1v1 perspective gathering.
   - Synthesis report screenshot/mock.
   - Dashboard/status cards.
   - Member progression teaser.

7. **CTA**
   - “Tell us what decision your group is stuck on.”
   - Chat-style lead capture should ask: group size, current decision, how input is gathered now, what happens when people disagree.

---

## Copy Rules

- Prefer concrete pains over abstract “alignment” language.
- Use numbers: 200 members, 35 at town hall, 23 poll votes, 2 questions, 10 minutes.
- Avoid generic SaaS language: “collaboration,” “stakeholder engagement,” “unlock insights.”
- Lead with relief: less chasing, less guessing, fewer meetings, fewer legitimacy fights.
- Show that Zolara changes the decision process, not just the report format.
